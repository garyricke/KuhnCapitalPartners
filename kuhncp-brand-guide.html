01 — Color System

Color Palette

White is the dominant surface — not navy. Navy is the authority accent. Gold is the single warm signal. This is the Ritholtz model: white canvas with dark and gold as deliberate interruptions.

White (Canvas)
The dominant page background. Most sections, most cards, most surfaces. This is what the page breathes.
#FFFFFE · RGB 255,255,254
KCP Navy
Authority accent. Navigation, hero, select sections, callout blocks. Used intentionally — not as a default.
#022C58 · RGB 2,44,88
KCP Gold
The only warm accent. CTAs, dividers, eyebrows, active states. One application per visual hierarchy level.
#FFB921 · RGB 255,185,33
Sky Blue
Section background accent (Ritholtz uses as a full section bg). Tags, data highlights, secondary color moments.
#B4E2FF · RGB 180,226,255
Off-White
Alternate light surface. Card backgrounds within white sections, table stripes, subtle section differentiation.
#F5F7FA · RGB 245,247,250
Near Black
Body text on white surfaces. Deep background for hero-within-hero moments. Button text on gold CTAs.
#151515 · RGB 21,21,21
White Is The Default
The page body background is white (#FFFFFE). Navy sections are the accent — used intentionally 2–3 times per page for authority moments. Most content lives on white. This matches Ritholtz's actual layout.
Navy As Interruption
Navy sections are placed where maximum visual weight is needed — hero, a key stat section, footer. They are not the default canvas. When everything is dark, nothing stands out. White gives navy its power.
Gold Is Still Reserved
On white backgrounds: gold CTAs, gold eyebrow labels, gold top-border on stat cards. The muted charcoal text lets gold read loudly. Never multiple gold elements competing in the same zone.
Sky Blue As Section Bg
Sky blue (#B4E2FF) can be used as a full section background — Ritholtz does this for service tier blocks. On sky blue: navy headings, charcoal body text. Never use gold text on sky blue.

Zero Serif
No Playfair, no Georgia, no Times. Rubik for display, Rambla for body. The geometric sans identity is the brand — no exceptions.
All-Caps via CSS
Hero and section headlines use text-transform: uppercase applied in CSS — not manually typed. Rubik Black 900 at 96% line-height.
Body Spec (Ritholtz exact)
Rambla Regular, 21px, 157% line-height on desktop. 18px on mobile (max-width: 768px). These values are adopted verbatim.
Button Spec (Ritholtz exact)
Rambla Bold, 24px, uppercase, letter-spacing 0.08em. No lighter weight, no mixed case on CTA buttons.
Fluid Display Sizing
Hero text: clamp(16px, 5vw, 96px) with 96% line-height — the exact Ritholtz spec. Never a fixed px size for hero or section display text.
Line Length
Body prose capped at 65ch max-width. Unconstrained full-width prose blocks are a design failure. Apply max-width to all paragraph containers.

06 — Voice & Tone

Brand Voice

KCP speaks the way Ritholtz speaks — with earned confidence. Direct, substantive, no hype. Not polished for its own sake. The voice of a firm that has done the work.

Direct
Declarative sentences. No weasel words. State the point, move on.
Blunt
"No hype. No shortcuts." is a brand promise, not a tagline. Mean it in every sentence.
Honest
Acknowledge risk. Acknowledge fees. Trust is earned through transparency, not enthusiasm.
Bold
Lead with "WHERE ARE YOU GOING?" — not "We're here to help." Make statements. Take a posture.
Write This
"No hype. No shortcuts. Your interest, always first."
Short. Declarative. Fiduciary promise in one line.
"Where are you going? We can take you there."
Ritholtz-style opener. Interrogative, then confident.
"We manage wealth for business owners who want one relationship, not five vendors."
Specific, differentiated, respects the reader's intelligence.
"No commissions. One fee, clearly disclosed. Every time."
Short punchy fragments land harder than full sentences here.
Never Write This
"We leverage cutting-edge, best-in-class solutions to empower your financial journey."
Buzzword soup. Could be any firm. Start over.
"At KCP, we're passionate about helping you achieve your dreams!"
Exclamation points. Vague emotion. Zero substance.
"Our comprehensive suite of holistic, tailored solutions is designed to meet your unique needs."
Every word is meaningless. Say what you actually do.
"Results may vary. Past performance is not indicative of future results."
Legal boilerplate belongs in the footer, never in brand copy.
