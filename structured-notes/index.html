What Is a Structured Note?

Bank-Issued. Defined Outcomes. Built for Serious Investors.

A structured note is a bank-issued financial instrument that packages fixed-income characteristics with market-linked returns — giving you the opportunity for double-digit yields while limiting how much you can lose.

Unlike stocks, you know the rules before you invest. You know the coupon rate, the observation dates, the downside buffer, and the maturity date. No guessing. No surprises.

These are not boutique products. Structured notes are issued by JPMorgan (A1/A-), Goldman Sachs, Citigroup, and Barclays — the same institutions that manage trillions in pension and endowment money. Until recently, access was limited to institutional investors. Kuhn Capital Partners brings this strategy to individual investors.

01

You Invest

A minimum investment buys you a note issued by a major bank, linked to an underlying index (S&P 500, Russell 2000, or similar).

02

Monthly Coupons Paid

Each month, if the underlying assets are at or above the coupon protection barrier, you receive your monthly coupon — up to 15.45% annually.

03

Autocall or Maturity

The note can autocall after 6 months (returning your principal at par), or run the full 60-month term. If markets drop below 30%, protection applies at maturity.
