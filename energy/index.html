The Mechanics

How Oil & Gas Tax Investments Work

The IRS allows investors in domestic oil and gas production to deduct a substantial portion of their investment — in the same year it's made. Here's the simple version.

01

You Invest

As an accredited investor, you participate directly in domestic oil and gas drilling operations. Your capital funds actual well development — not a fund of funds.

$50K+
Typical minimum investment
02

Intangible Costs Are Deducted

80–90% of your investment consists of Intangible Drilling Costs (IDC) — labor, fuel, chemicals, rig time — which are 100% deductible in the year they occur.

80–90%
Deducted in year one
03

You Reduce Your Tax Bill

The IDC deduction offsets your ordinary income — W-2, business income, or passive income depending on your structure. Depletion allowances continue in years two and beyond.

15–27.5%
Ongoing depletion allowance
Accredited Investors Only

Who This Is Right For

Oil and gas direct participation isn't for everyone. It requires accredited investor status and a meaningful tax burden to make the deduction count. Here's who benefits most.

Schedule a Call to Qualify →

Physicians & Surgeons

High W-2 income with limited deductions. Oil & gas participation offsets earned income dollar-for-dollar.

Business Owners

Business owners with pass-through income of $300K+ can shelter a meaningful portion of their taxable income.

Real Estate Investors

Passive activity rules may allow oil & gas losses to offset real estate income — consult your tax advisor.

Liquidity Events

Just sold a business or property? A large capital gain year is often the perfect time for a meaningful energy investment.
